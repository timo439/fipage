Does This Sound Familiar?

01

You're the human fire extinguisher

Every problem lands on your desk. Your team won't make a decision without you. You're not running a business — you're managing a permanent emergency. Your day is just a series of things that came up.

"I can't even take a week off without it falling apart."

02

You get customers — but they churn

You're working hard to win clients, but the back door is wide open. Retention is terrible and you know it. You're constantly replacing revenue instead of building on top of it — and it's exhausting.

"I never know what next month is going to look like."

03

You attract sh*tty clients

The clients you're landing want everything for nothing. Small budgets, big demands, slow decisions. You know the big boys — the Estée Lauders, the Huaweis — are out there. You just can't seem to get in front of them.

"I keep getting small, bad clients. I want the big boys."

04

You hired people — but you're still doing everything

You thought hiring would free you. Instead you're supervising people who need supervising, or terrified of losing the one person who actually delivers. You're managing, not leading.

"I'm spending more time managing the team than doing anything useful."

05

You want to sell — but have no clue where to start

You know you want to exit at some point. But when you actually try to think about how — valuations, buyers, what makes a company acquirable — you hit a wall. Nobody taught you this part.

"I want to sell my business. But how the f*ck do I even start?"
